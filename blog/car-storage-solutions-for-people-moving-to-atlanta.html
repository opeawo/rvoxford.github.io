Car Storage Solutions for People Moving to Atlanta

Moving to a new city creates a period of transition where your vehicle situation may not match your housing situation. Maybe you're living in temporary housing that can't accommodate your car. Maybe you're arriving before your permanent place is ready. Maybe you're bringing a second vehicle that won't fit at your new apartment. Whatever the reason, new Atlanta residents often need short-term vehicle storage.

Common Moving-Related Storage Scenarios

Temporary Housing Gap

You're staying in an Airbnb, corporate housing, or with friends while finding permanent housing. Parking may be limited, insecure, or nonexistent.

Typical duration: 2-8 weeks

Storage need: Secure parking until your permanent place has appropriate parking.

Apartment Without Adequate Parking

Many Atlanta apartments—especially in desirable neighborhoods like Midtown, Virginia-Highland, or East Atlanta—have limited parking. One car might fit; two definitely won't.

Typical duration: Until your living situation changes

Storage need: Long-term solution for the second vehicle.

House Purchase Timeline

You've sold your previous home but the new house closing is delayed. You need somewhere to keep your vehicles during the gap.

Typical duration: 2-6 weeks (hopefully)

Storage need: Short-term bridge storage.

Extra Vehicles During Transition

You're bringing vehicles you won't immediately need—a classic car, RV, boat, or seasonal vehicle. Your new housing doesn't have space for them.

Typical duration: Ongoing until you have appropriate space

Storage need: Long-term storage for specialty vehicles.

What New Residents Need to Know

Atlanta Geography for Storage

Inside I-285 (the Perimeter):

  • Higher storage costs
  • Limited availability for large vehicles
  • Convenient if you're living/working in-town

Outside I-285:

  • More affordable
  • More space and options for larger vehicles
  • Less convenient for daily access

For temporary storage during a move, convenience often matters more than cost. For long-term specialty vehicle storage, cost savings outside the Perimeter make more sense.

Month-to-Month Is Essential

During a move, timelines are uncertain. Don't commit to 6-month or annual contracts when you don't know your end date. Pay a bit more for month-to-month flexibility.

Location Relative to Your Situation

Choose storage that makes sense for your current situation:

  • Near your temporary housing for easy access
  • Near your workplace if you're commuting during the transition
  • Near your future home if you have a solid timeline

Access Hours Matter

During a move, you may need your car at odd times. Confirm the facility has hours that work for your needs.

Storage Options for Movers

Standard Vehicle Storage Facilities

Purpose-built for vehicle storage. Good for cars, trucks, and some larger vehicles.

Pros:

  • Designed for vehicles
  • Gated access and security
  • Various options (outdoor, covered, indoor)

Cons:

  • May have minimum stay requirements
  • Administrative fees for short stays

RV/Boat Storage Facilities

For RVs, boats, trailers, or larger vehicles you're bringing to Atlanta.

Pros:

  • Space for larger vehicles
  • Often more flexible on terms
  • Usually cheaper for big rigs than general storage

Cons:

  • May be farther from in-town locations

Hotel Valet Parking

Some Atlanta hotels offer parking for non-guests, especially if you're staying at their property during your transition.

Pros:

  • Convenient
  • Security of hotel parking garage

Cons:

  • Expensive for long-term
  • Limited to certain hotels

Private Arrangements

Renting space from a private individual through Craigslist, Facebook, or word of mouth.

Pros:

  • Often very affordable
  • Sometimes more flexible

Cons:

  • Variable security
  • No guarantees

Cost Expectations

For temporary storage during a move:

Inside I-285:

  • Cars: $100-175/month
  • RVs/large vehicles: $150-300/month

Outside I-285:

  • Cars: $60-125/month
  • RVs/large vehicles: $100-200/month

Plus possible admin/setup fees ($25-50), especially for short stays.

Practical Tips for Movers

Before You Arrive

  1. Research storage options in the areas you're considering living
  2. Get quotes and understand terms
  3. Reserve space if needed (especially for large vehicles)

Logistics

  1. Plan how you'll get from storage to your temporary/permanent housing
  2. Uber/Lyft are readily available throughout Atlanta
  3. MARTA (public transit) covers many areas if you're near a station

Documentation

  1. Take photos of your vehicle before storing
  2. Keep storage facility contact info accessible
  3. Set a reminder to check on your vehicle periodically

Insurance

  1. Verify your insurance covers the car while in storage
  2. Update your insurance with your new Georgia address when appropriate
  3. Georgia requires continuous insurance coverage on registered vehicles

When Your Situation Stabilizes

Once you're settled:

  • Re-evaluate whether you still need storage
  • Consider moving to a facility more convenient to your permanent home
  • Evaluate whether monthly storage costs make sense vs. alternatives

Oxford RV Storage for New Residents

Oxford RV Storage is located about 40 miles east of Atlanta along I-20. For new residents settling in the east Metro (Conyers, Covington, Stonecrest area), it's a convenient local option. For those settling elsewhere in Atlanta, it may or may not make sense depending on your specific situation.

For temporary housing transitions, facilities closer to your temporary location are usually more practical. For long-term specialty vehicle storage, Oxford's lower costs may justify the distance regardless of where you end up living.
