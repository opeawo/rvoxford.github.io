Types of Industrial Storage

Outdoor Equipment/Vehicle Storage

Open or covered parking for:

  • Work trucks and vans
  • Equipment trailers
  • Construction equipment (weight permitting)
  • Company vehicles

Cost range: $75-300/month per vehicle/space

Drive-Up Storage Units

Self-storage units with vehicle access:

  • Equipment that fits through a rollup door
  • Inventory and supplies
  • Tools and smaller equipment

Cost range: $150-500/month depending on size

Warehouse Space

Dedicated building space:

  • Inventory storage
  • Manufacturing support
  • Distribution operations

Cost range: $6-15/sq ft annually (varies widely)

Flex Space

Combination office/warehouse:

  • Small business operations
  • Light manufacturing
  • Distribution with admin

Cost range: $8-18/sq ft annually

What Makes Industrial Storage "Affordable"

Location Trade-offs

Inside I-285: Premium pricing, limited availability

Just outside Perimeter: Moderate savings (15-25% less)

Outer suburbs (I-20 corridor, south/north exurbs): Significant savings (30-50% less)

Facility Type

Purpose-built industrial parks: Higher quality, higher cost

Converted/older buildings: Often more affordable

Open yard storage: Lowest cost for equipment/vehicles

Commitment Terms

Month-to-month: Highest rates, maximum flexibility

Annual lease: 10-20% savings typically

Multi-year commitment: May negotiate additional discounts

Finding Affordable Options

For Vehicles and Equipment

RV/Boat storage facilities: Often accommodate work vehicles at competitive rates

Commercial yards: Serve contractors specifically

Industrial property with excess space: Can be negotiated directly

For Inventory and Goods

Traditional self-storage: Works for smaller quantities

Shared warehouse space: Split larger spaces with other businesses

Sublease opportunities: Businesses with excess space

For Business Operations

Incubator/flex space: Lower rates for smaller footprints

Older industrial parks: Lower rents than modern facilities

Emerging industrial areas: Growing areas often have competitive rates

Geographic Options Around Atlanta

I-20 East Corridor

Areas: Conyers, Covington, Oxford, Social Circle

Characteristics:

  • Growing industrial/warehouse presence
  • Competitive pricing
  • Good I-20 access

Best for: Businesses serving east Metro, companies prioritizing cost

South Metro

Areas: Newnan, Peachtree City, Fayetteville, McDonough

Characteristics:

  • Mix of established and developing areas
  • I-85 and I-75 access
  • Variable pricing by specific location

Best for: Distribution, south-side based operations

North Metro

Areas: Buford, Suwanee, Lawrenceville, Norcross

Characteristics:

  • More developed industrial base
  • Higher pricing than south or east
  • Good transportation access

Best for: Tech, distribution, established businesses

West Metro

Areas: Douglasville, Villa Rica, Carrollton

Characteristics:

  • Growing industrial presence
  • Competitive pricing
  • I-20 west access

Best for: Manufacturing, distribution, cost-conscious businesses

Evaluating Industrial Storage

For Vehicle/Equipment Storage

  • Surface quality (can it handle your equipment?)
  • Security (appropriate for your investment)
  • Access hours (match your operational needs)
  • Maneuvering space

For Building Space

  • Ceiling height (sufficient for your operations)
  • Loading dock access
  • Power availability
  • Condition of space
  • Landlord responsiveness

For All Types

  • Lease terms and flexibility
  • Total cost (including utilities, fees, etc.)
  • Location fit for your business
  • Growth or contraction ability

Cost Comparison Example

Scenario: Small contractor needing vehicle storage + small warehouse

Option A: In-town Atlanta

  • 2 vehicle spaces: $200/month each = $400
  • 500 sq ft unit: $400/month
  • Total: $800/month, $9,600/year

Option B: I-20 East Corridor

  • 2 vehicle spaces: $120/month each = $240
  • 500 sq ft unit: $250/month
  • Total: $490/month, $5,880/year

Annual savings: $3,720

This doesn't account for any time/travel differences, but illustrates the cost gap.
